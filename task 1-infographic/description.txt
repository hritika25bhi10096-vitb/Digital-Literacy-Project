I used Canva to create the "Digital Literacy" infographic, which is a professional, well-organized guide for students. To make the information reedable and interesting, the desing combines a simple, fivestep numerical layout with simple illustrations and a unified cream-and-mustard color scheme.
The difficulty of summarizing the "Digital Footprint" section caught my attention. Condensing the complicated concept of the longterm online reputation into a straightforward "think before you post" reminder was a creative exercise. This procedure demonstrated how visual design depends just as much on what is left out as it does on what is included.
